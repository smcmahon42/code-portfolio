Hitting all the right
notes for whiplash

The soundscape mix for Damien Chazelle’s dramatic feature, Whiplash, was created at Technicolor’s theatrical mix stages at Paramount Studios. The film – about a young jazz drummer so obsessed with his craft that he willingly subjects himself to the sadistic training methods of his even more obsessive conductor – has been receiving noticeable praise. For their incredible work on Whiplash, supervising sound mixers Ben Wilkins, Craig Mann, and Thomas Curley won a BAFTA, an Oscar and other industry awards.

This is What State of the Art Sound Looks Like

A global offering across many of our locations, our 650,000 square foot sound facility on the Paramount lot is our piece-de-resistance as a world class post production facility in size, seamless technology and scope. It features eight remixing stages with surrounding editing rooms and producer lounges, eight sound design rooms, 16 sound editorial rooms, three large ADR stages, a Foley stage and more.

Helping Birdman soar to incredible heights

Alejandro González Iñárritu's Oscar-winning picture, Birdman - the tale of a has-been movie star and his ego-driven attempt to mount a comeback Broadway play - creates a totally original illusion that suggests the entire movie was shot in a single, never-ending take. The team at Technicolor collaborated with the cinematographer and visual effects vendor to create this cinematic wizardry through the incredibly complex and innovative digital intermediate and final conforming process for a the film. We even invented a new methodology for layering final color down on top of the movie's final cut. The result is nothing less than amazing.
