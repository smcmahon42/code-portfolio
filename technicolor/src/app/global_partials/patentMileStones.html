• Introduction of Optical Printer - 1944

    Optical Printer introduced by Technicolor to enable fades, dissolves and special effects to be printed in color

  • RCA Corners Color TV Market - 1953-54

    RCA's all-electronic, compatible TV system was declared the US standard in December 1953 (known as NTSC), and 1954 saw the first RCA Color TV in market (RCA/Thomson)

  • Technicolor founds MPEG - 1988

    Introducing MPEG

  • Satellite Broadcasting - 1993

    Technicolor enables satellite broadcasters to transmit hundreds of channels to an 18” rooftop dish antenna throughout the continental US by adapting the MPEG video compression

  • MP3 Licensing program Launched - 1993

    The MP3 audio format, which Technicolor invented, becomes an ISO standard and in 1995 we launched the MP3 Licensing program with Fraunhofer Institute, fueling MP3's dramatic expansion in the distribution of music and internet broadcasting

  • MPEG2 standard is introduced to the market and supported by Technicolor - 1996

    MPEG2 standard is introduced to the market and supported by Technicolor

  • World’s first real-time streaming of uncompressed digital 4K movie over long distance IP network - 2006

    Creating the future of streaming movies.

  • Technicolor introduces Qeo Technology - 2014

    Technicolor brings Qeo Technology, a revolutionary software offering consumer device manufacturers, developers and service providers a common language to offer a truly operable Connected Life

  • Technicolor becomes Premier level member of the AllSeen Alliance - 2014

    Alliance further unifies the Internet of Everything (IoE) ecosystem

  • Technicolor becomes founding member of the UHD Alliance - 2014

    Technicolor establish premium quality standards across content and devices
