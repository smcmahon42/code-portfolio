• Technicolor Process #1

    1917

    Technicolor Process #1 is used for the first time on the film The Gulf Between

  • 1st Hollywood Laboratory

    1923

    Technicolor’s first Hollywood Laboratory established, the largest of its time devoted to color

  • First animated theatrical cartoon short serviced in full color

    1932

    Walt Disney’s Flower & Trees. The project later won Disney's first Oscar & Technicolor receives its first Scientific & Technical Achievement Academy Award for color cartoon process

  • Gone With The Wind

    1939

    Gone With The Wind was first full feature length motion picture in Technicolor to be awarded the Academy Award for Color Cinematography

  • The Wizard of Oz

    1939

    The Wizard of Oz. First three strip IB dye-transfer color process is used in this historic film and Dr. Herbert Kalmus is awarded a special Oscar for "successfully bringing three-color feature production to the screen"

  • Academy “Sci-Tech” Award for Technicolor RATS innovation

    2005

    Award for RATS (Real Time Answer Print Timing) for laboratory color pre-visualization

  • Academy "Sci-Tech" Award for Technicolor "digital YCM" color archival process

    2006

  • Technicolor wins IBC Innovation Award for development of the company's first DP lights system

    2006

  • Color Scientists awarded Academy Sci-tech Award

    2014

    Technicolor Color Scientists awarded Academy Sci-tech Award for contributions to the American Society of Cinematographer’s Color Decision List
