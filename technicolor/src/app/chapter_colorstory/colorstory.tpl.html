Color is not just in our company name – it’s in our DNA. We’ve been immortalized as the brains behind the vibrant hues that wowed audiences worldwide when color first flooded into the cinema. And even today, no company has more depth of talent and experience in color than Technicolor.

Color Milestones

The story of color in motion pictures and entertainment is filled with so important chapters. Here are just a few of our most notable achievements.

OUR NAME ENDS WITH COLOR

1939 was truly a memorable year for our company, with two iconic American films released in 3-strip Technicolor – Gone With The Wind and The Wizard Of Oz. It was also the year we won our first in a long line of Academy Awards for Color Cinematography. Since then, no company has contributed more to color motion picture production, finishing, distribution, as well as restoration and preservation. No wonder we’ve been awarded 24 Academy Scientific and Technical Achievement Awards.

OUR STORY BEGINS WITH Color

Technicolor was founded in 1915 with our first lab – a converted railroad car – and a two-color technique called Technicolor Process No. 1. In the years that followed, we created sharper images, improved color tones and introduced constant innovations that opened the door to a whole new world of color filmmaking possibilities.
